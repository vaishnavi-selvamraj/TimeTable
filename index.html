Academic Rules & Expectations

  1. Attendance: Maintain a minimum attendance percentage, which is mandatory to be eligible for examinations.
  2. Punctuality: Be on time for classes and other college activities.
  3. Assignments: Complete home assignments, tutorials, and tests, which are generally compulsory.
  4. Academic Integrity: Avoid plagiarism and any other form of academic malpractices.
  5. Classroom Behavior: Enter and leave classrooms only with the teacher's permission and maintain silence to avoid disturbing others.

Campus & Facility Rules

  • Identity Card: Always carry your identity card and produce it when requested by an authorized person.
  • Notice Boards: Check the notice boards regularly for important information, circulars, and notices regarding college activities and examinations.
  • Prohibited Areas: Do not enter college offices or staff rooms unless authorized or during specified hours.
  • Lost Items: Promptly report any lost or found articles to the college office.
  • Unauthorized Gatherings: Do not organize any meetings or entertainment events without prior permission from the Principal.

Behavioral & Conduct Rules

  1. Dress Code: Wear clean, neat, and modest attire as prescribed by the institution.
  2. Respect: Be courteous and polite in language and conduct toward fellow students, senior students, and college faculty and staff.
  3. Prohibited Activities: Do not smoke, consume alcohol, or use drugs or other intoxicants on the college campus.
  4. Damaging Property: Do not damage college property, furniture, walls, or any other college fixtures.
  5. Ragging: Engaging in or abetting ragging is strictly prohibited and carries severe penalties, including dismissal and legal action.
  6. Mobile Phones: Do not use mobile phones in classrooms, laboratories, or the library.

Consequences of Non-Compliance

  • Disciplinary Action: Misbehavior, irregular attendance, or breaking other rules can lead to disciplinary action.
  • Penalties: These actions can include suspension, dismissal from college, recovery of damages, or even criminal charges.
